Augmented Reality (AR) & Virtual Reality (VR)

Purpose

The main purpose of Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) is to create immersive, interactive experiences by blending virtual content with the real world (AR) or by transporting users entirely into simulated environments (VR). These technologies aim to enhance learning, training, visualization, and collaboration, making abstract concepts more tangible and complex processes easier to understand.

Reference:
Milgram, P., & Kishino, F. (2022). “A taxonomy of mixed reality visual displays.” Presence: Teleoperators & Virtual Environments. MIT Press.
Retrieved from https://doi.org/10.1162/pres.1994.3.2.132

Usage

In Education
AR and VR are transforming education by enabling immersive learning experiences.
For example, VR simulations can place students inside historical events, scientific labs, or remote locations, making learning more engaging and memorable. AR tools overlay digital annotations or 3D models onto textbooks, classrooms, or lab setups to provide interactive, real-time information.

Example: The University of Maryland uses VR in its journalism program to let students produce immersive stories, while apps like Google Expeditions allow virtual field trips to museums, outer space, or underwater ecosystems.

Reference:
Johnson, L. (2023). “Immersive Technologies in the Classroom: The Rise of AR and VR.” EdTech Magazine. Retrieved from https://www.edtechmagazine.com/k12/article/2023/05/immersive-learning

In IT
In IT, AR/VR are used for remote collaboration, prototyping, and simulation. VR environments support virtual meetings where engineers can visualize 3D models together in real time, while AR assists technicians by overlaying step-by-step guides during complex hardware maintenance.
They are also widely used in cybersecurity training simulations, allowing IT professionals to practice responses to cyber incidents in safe virtual environments.

Example: Microsoft HoloLens 2 is used by IT teams to visualize network architecture and guide on-site hardware installations with live AR instructions.

Reference:
Microsoft (2024). “HoloLens 2 in Enterprise IT.” Retrieved from https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/hololens/enterprise

Real-World Examples

In Education
Case: VictoryXR Academy creates VR-based campuses for remote learning, where students attend virtual classrooms, conduct science experiments, and collaborate with peers in simulated spaces.

Reference:
VictoryXR (2023). “Building Virtual Campuses for Education.” Retrieved from https://www.victoryxr.com/

In IT
Case: Accenture uses its “Nth Floor” VR platform for onboarding new employees, providing them with a shared virtual office environment regardless of location. This platform allows for IT project discussions, system design reviews, and collaborative brainstorming.

Reference:
Accenture (2023). “The Nth Floor: VR for the Future of Work.” Retrieved from https://www.accenture.com/us-en/insights/future-work/virtual-office
